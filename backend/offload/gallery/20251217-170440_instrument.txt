# Summary

**Conceptual Summary**

This speculative instrument merges idiophonic, membranophonic, and aerophonic principles into a single resonant body. The organogram abstracts the main resonant chamber as a vertical rectangle (chordophone genus, indicating a tensioned skin) with a cylindrical bezel (a circle, representing an aerophone tube) attached. A sphere (a circle, representing a secondary resonant chamber) is connected at one point to the cylinder. An arrow flows from the sphere to a smaller circle on its surface, representing the interactive "finger hole" inspired by the Udu drum, where pitch and timbre are modulated by opening/closing. The primary rectangle is colored pink (skin), the cylinder and sphere are beige (gourd/ceramic), and the hole interface is marked in orange with an 'R' for relative pitch control. The acoustical rationale is a Helmholtz-like system: the main chamber provides a low fundamental, the cylinder acts as a tuned port, and the spherical chamber with its variable hole creates a coupled resonator for complex, water-like tones. The performer interacts by striking the skin (primary excitation) and modulating the sphere's hole (secondary articulation).

**Materials**

1.  1 ceramic or hardened clay spherical chamber, 150 mm diameter, with a 20 mm diameter opening for connection.
2.  1 ceramic or PVC cylindrical tube, 80 mm length, 40 mm diameter, to connect sphere to main body.
3.  1 wooden or fiberglass frame (main chamber box), 300 mm height, 200 mm width, 150 mm depth.
4.  1 synthetic drum skin (membrane), 250 mm diameter, for the main striking surface.
5.  Synthetic lacing and tuning rings to tension the membrane.
6.  Adhesive (epoxy or strong clay adhesive) to join sphere and cylinder.

**Virtual Materials** (for digital/physical hybrid version)
1.  A sample library of struck membrane and variable Helmholtz resonator tones (WAV, 48kHz).
2.  A proximity sensor (virtual asset) mapped to the sphere's hole for real-time modulation in a digital audio workstation.
3.  A procedural texture simulating glazed ceramic for 3D model rendering.

fig.patch.set_alpha(0.0)
ax.set_facecolor('none')

ax.add_patch(semi_circle)
