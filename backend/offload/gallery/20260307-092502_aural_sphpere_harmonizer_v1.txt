# Summary

### Conceptual Summary

The instrument is named "Aural Sphere Harmonizer." It features a spherical membrane as its idiophone core, with a speaker at its center for electronic augmentation. Two aerophones (tubes) extend from opposite sides of the sphere, each containing three holes and bessels. Microphones are placed at the points where the tubes meet the sphere to capture internal sound. The spheres represent isotropic resonance, while the tubes enhance air flow and provide additional acoustical feedback through their multiple openings.

The organogram will have:
- A central sphere (idiophone) with a speaker in the center.
- Two aerophones extending from opposite sides of the sphere, each with three holes.
- Microphones at the points where the tubes meet the sphere.
- Arrows indicating air flow and signal paths from microphones to speakers.
