# Summary

**Conceptual Summary**

The organogram visualizes a speculative xylophone with bars made from acoustic topological insulators. The instrument is classified as an idiophone (golden rectangles), with a genus arc indicating it is struck. Each bar is internally patterned with a hexagonal lattice (cyan) representing the insulating phononic crystal structure, which confines vibrational modes to the bar edges, enhancing sustain and purity. Orange interface patches and white arrows show the striking points and energy flow into the bar's topological edge states. Blue numbers label the 12 graduated bars. The performer position (white circle) and interaction lines illustrate the mallet's path. The design merges traditional idiophone geometry with material science, creating an instrument where the bar's internal architecture, not just its dimensions, dictates its acoustical spectrum.

**Materials**

1.  12 Acoustic topological insulator bars: 3D-printed photopolymer resin with hexagonal lattice infill (20% density). Dimensions vary from 300 x 40 x 15 mm to 150 x 40 x 15 mm.
2.  2 Rubber isolation feet: 30 mm diameter, 10 mm height.
3.  1 Frame: Aluminum square tube, 1200 mm length, 25 mm side.
4.  2 End supports: CNC-milled plywood, 150 x 50 x 20 mm.
5.  2 Hard rubber mallets (interface).
6.  **Virtual Materials**: Hexagonal lattice voxel grid for FEA simulation; Impulse response sample set for each bar (48 kHz, 24-bit).
