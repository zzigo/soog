# Summary

**Conceptual Summary**

This organogram visualizes a speculative hybrid instrument merging a classical guitar (chordophone) with digital and kinetic elements. The guitar is abstracted as a vertical orange rectangle, its six strings numbered in blue, adhering to Hood's organological classification. A grey rhombus inside the body represents the integrated camera (electronophone), symbolizing digital capture. The lime-green fractal polygon signifies the algorithmically generated, immersive sonic environment projected *within* the performer, indicated by a white dotted-line circle. Orange arrows show the flow: camera input → fractal processor → haptic/audio output. Small silver circles denote neodymium-bearing balls, introducing chaotic, resonant percussion via magnetic interaction (shown with attraction/repulsion arrows). The design rationale is an embodied feedback loop: the performer's physical interaction with the guitar (strings, kinetic balls) is captured, transformed into a complex fractal soundscape, and fed back as a visceral, internalized auditory experience, blurring the interface between player, instrument, and sonic space.

**Materials**

1.  1 Acoustic guitar body (or build from orange wood), standard dimensions.
2.  6 Steel guitar strings (various gauges).
3.  1 Miniature USB endoscope camera, 5mm diameter.
4.  3 Neodymium magnet balls, 6mm diameter, nickel-plated.
5.  Raspberry Pi 4 (or similar) with audio HAT, for fractal audio synthesis.
6.  Piezo contact microphone for body resonance pickup.
7.  Solder, wiring, mounting hardware.
8.  Small, thin non-magnetic plate (e.g., acrylic) to mount balls inside body.

**Virtual Materials**
1.  Fractal noise synthesis patch (e.g., in Pure Data or Max/MSP).
2.  Real-time video-to-audio FFT processing shader.
3.  Impulse response library of metallic ball bearings on wood/glass.
