# Conceptual Summary

The organogram for the Quantum Walk Xylophone abstracts its resonant bars as vertical rectangles (chordophone-derived shapes) in a deep violet, representing the quantum metamaterial's crystalline lattice structure that manipulates phonon propagation. Each bar is overlaid with a shimmering, semi-transparent teal waveform polygon, symbolizing the non-local acoustic interference patterns generated by quantum walk algorithmsâ€”where sound probability amplitudes evolve in superposition. Arrows in gold indicate the performer's mallet strikes (interface: small orange circles with 'H'), which initiate not just a fundamental tone but a cascading, probabilistic harmonic cloud. Numbers in blue (e.g., 16 bars) denote measurable components. The performer's position is a white circle with a dotted line to the instrument, highlighting interactive control over quantum decoherence via strike velocity. This design merges idiophone tradition with speculative quantum acoustics, where geometry dictates a stochastic, spatially diffuse timbre.
