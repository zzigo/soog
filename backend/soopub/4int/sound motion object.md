A rapid harp glissando, a fast drum fill, a harpsicord ornament, a trumpet signal, or a tremolo crescendo on a cymbal are all examples of sound-motion objects in the sense that they combine salient sound energy envelopes with salient sound-producing body motion shapes into strongly coherent entities, typically in the duration range of 0.3–5 s. The main features of sound-motion objects are that they are multimodal, meaning that they include sensations of both sound and sound-producing body motion, and that they are conceived and perceived holistically as strongly coherent and stable units and hence may be called _objects_ in musical performance.

The idea of sound-motion objects stems from our research on music-related body motion (see [Godøy et al., 2016](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2021.732729/full#ref41) for an overview), but is derived from Pierre Schaeffer’s theory of _sound objects_ ([Schaeffer, 1998](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2021.732729/full#ref79), [2012](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2021.732729/full#ref80), [2017](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2021.732729/full#ref81); [Chion, 2009](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2021.732729/full#ref12); [Godøy, 2021](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2021.732729/full#ref36)).



Sound-motion objects are cohesive entities that combine both sound and sound-producing body motion. They are typically experienced as stable units and have a duration ranging from 0.3 to 5 seconds. Examples of sound-motion objects include a rapid harp glissando, a fast drum fill, a harpsichord ornament, a trumpet signal, or a tremolo crescendo on a cymbal.

The concept of sound-motion objects is based on research on music-related body motion and is influenced by Pierre Schaeffer's theory of sound objects. Sound-motion objects are considered multimodal because they incorporate sensations of both sound and the bodily movements that produce the sound. In musical performance, these objects are perceived and conceived holistically as coherent entities.
