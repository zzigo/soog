# Material Acoustic Atlas

Use these notes to couple material choices with geometry decisions in organogram-to-STL generation.

## Core Mapping

- wood: medium damping, warm spectrum, stable body resonance.
- bamboo: light structure, directional airflow, fast response.
- metal: low damping, bright partials, strong projection.
- skin/membrane: transient-heavy attack, short sustain.
- plastic: controlled damping, lightweight housing.
- glass/stone/ceramic: rigid, bright but brittle behavior.
- string/fiber: tension-driven modes, narrow resonant elements.

## Geometry Implications

- high-rigidity materials (metal, glass, stone): thinner walls, longer resonators, sharper edges.
- medium-rigidity materials (wood, bamboo): moderate wall thickness, balanced cavity proportion.
- high-damping materials (skin, rubber, soft plastics): thicker shells and shorter cavities.
- string-based systems: elongated thin components and bridge/coupling elements.

## Acoustic Targets

- lower fundamentals: increase effective resonator length and cavity volume.
- brighter timbre: increase stiffness cues and reduce damping cues.
- softer timbre: increase damping cues and reduce edge sharpness.
